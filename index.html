☯ 6,000 Years of First Medicine™

"The superior physician treats disease before it occurs. The inferior physician treats disease after it has occurred."
— Huangdi Neijing (Yellow Emperor's Classic), circa 2600 BCE

Traditional Chinese Medicine is the world's oldest continuously practised medical system. For over 6,000 years — long before the first hospital, the first pharmacy, or the first clinical trial — healers were cataloguing the therapeutic properties of plants and developing a complete system of medicine based on careful observation of the human body.

The foundations of TCM:

  • Qi (氣) — The vital energy that flows through meridian channels in the body. When Qi flows freely, health is maintained. When it stagnates or becomes deficient, illness follows.
  • Yin and Yang (陰陽) — The two complementary forces that govern all phenomena. Health is dynamic balance. Illness is imbalance.
  • The Five Elements (五行) — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water. Each governs organ systems, emotions, seasons, and tastes. TCM reads the body as an interconnected whole.
  • Ben and Biao (本標) — The root cause (ben) and the presenting symptoms (biao). TCM treats both simultaneously. Western medicine typically treats the biao alone.
  • The Organ Systems — TCM identifies 12 primary organ systems, each with physical, mental, and emotional dimensions far beyond their Western anatomical equivalents.

The Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing — the Divine Farmer's Materia Medica, written around 2700 BCE — catalogued 365 medicinal herbs. This text forms the foundation of Chinese herbal medicine that is still practiced clinically today.

Modern science is now validating what TCM knew for millennia. Saffron comparable to antidepressants. Lavender comparable to benzodiazepines. Turmeric inhibiting the same inflammatory pathways as ibuprofen. Ginseng activating adaptogenic responses now understood at the molecular level.
